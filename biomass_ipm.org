#+BEGIN_abstract
Corn stover interactions involving cob fractions were found to contain the largest peaks and variance in friction coefficients (Table 3).
Cob particles with woody-ring sub-fractions on the contacting surface were observed to be significantly harder and more 'claw-like' in nature.
In contrast, the pith region of the cob was very similar to the exposed pith in stalk particles. Both stalk and husk particles showed moderate friction coefficients.
From the observation that cob particles show a larger standard deviation in the determined coefficient of friction, it can be hypothesized that cob particles will have a high contribution to erratic feedstock flow behavior.
